# Customer Discovery Script — The Mom Test Framework

## Before You Start
- Never mention your product idea until the very end (or not at all)
- You are here to LEARN, not to pitch
- Record every conversation (with permission) or take notes immediately after
- Target: SMB owners in healthcare, legal, finance, or e-commerce (5–50 employees)

---

## The Script

### Opening (30 seconds)
> "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time. I'm doing research on how small businesses handle cybersecurity — not selling anything, just trying to understand the problem better. Would you be okay if I asked you a few questions about your experience?"

---

### Core Questions (10–15 minutes)

**1. Understand their current situation:**
> "Can you walk me through how your business currently handles security? Like, what do you actually have in place day-to-day?"

*Listen for: antivirus, IT person, "we don't really do anything", compliance tools*

**2. Find the pain:**
> "Have you ever had a security scare — like an employee clicking something suspicious, or getting a weird email, or hearing about a breach at a company like yours?"

*Listen for: phishing attempts, ransomware fears, "my accountant got hacked", compliance anxiety*

**3. Measure intensity:**
> "How much does this keep you up at night, on a scale of 1–10?"

*If below 5: this person may not be your customer. If 7+: you found pain.*

**4. Understand current behavior:**
> "What have you done about it so far? Did you look into any tools or services?"

*Listen for: why they didn't buy existing solutions (too expensive, too complex, didn't trust it)*

**5. Find the budget signal:**
> "If something like this did happen — a breach, ransomware, data leak — what would that cost your business roughly?"

*Listen for: "it would destroy us", specific dollar amounts, insurance mentions*

**6. The dream outcome:**
> "In a perfect world, what would 'feeling secure' look like for your business? What would have to be true?"

*Listen for: "just knowing someone is watching", "simple alerts I actually understand", "not having to think about it"*

---

### Closing (2 minutes)
> "This has been really helpful. Based on what you've told me, I'm actually building something that might be relevant. It's an AI tool that [one sentence description]. Does that sound like something that would be useful to you?"

**If yes:** "Would you be open to seeing an early version and giving me feedback? I'd even offer you a founding member price."

**If no:** "Totally fair — can I ask what's missing for it to be useful?"

---

## Scoring Each Interview

After every call, score it:

| Signal | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Pain intensity 7+ | +3 |
| Had a real security incident | +2 |
| Currently paying for something (even basic) | +2 |
| Mentioned a specific dollar cost of a breach | +2 |
| Said "I wish I had something like that" unprompted | +3 |
| Asked when it would be ready | +2 |
| **Total possible** | **14** |

**8+ points = strong ICP fit. Book a follow-up.**
**Below 5 = wrong customer. Move on.**

---

## Where to Find People to Interview

**LinkedIn:** Search "Office Manager", "Operations Director", "Practice Manager" at companies with 10–50 employees in healthcare or legal.

**Cold message template:**
> "Hi [Name] — I'm doing research on how small [healthcare/legal/accounting] practices handle cybersecurity. No pitch, just 15 minutes of your time in exchange for honest research. Worth a quick chat?"

**Reddit:** Post in r/smallbusiness, r/legaladvice, r/medicalpractice — ask genuine questions, offer to share findings.

**Goal:** 10 conversations in 14 days.
